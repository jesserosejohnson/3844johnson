Artifacts

T-Shaped Podcast

The podcast was the first of the two group assignments in this class and focused on the “enhanced T” aspect of the VT-shaped learner model. My group and I chose to address this topic through the story of a fictional Creative Writing major who, despite finding her Writing and Digital Media class pointless for someone in her major in the beginning, eventually not only recognizes its value, but uses her newfound skills to hack NASA and publish her poetry. Through this story, we hoped to show that, while the VT-shaped student model might at first glance appear to be aimed at STEM majors, it is equally applicable to those in the liberal arts.

V-Video

After our success with the podcast, my group and I decided to follow a similar theme for our video about the “V” section of the VT-shaped student. However, rather than continue the plot of our podcast or create another fanciful storyline, we based this one more on reality and focused on three avenues that “utilized partnerships and student-life opportunities to advance formal and informal experiential learning“: internships, study abroad programs, and volunteer work in the community. We focused on how each was appropriate for various students’ circumstances and how advisors can assist in making these decisions if students choose to seek their advice.

An experiment.
